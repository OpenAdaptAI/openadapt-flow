# Compile-Reliability Study — diverse public web apps

_Does "record once, it generalizes" hold across varied real apps, or is every workflow bespoke?_

Generated by `scripts/reliability/run.py` over `openadapt_flow.benchmark.reliability_corpus`. Record -> compile -> replay-on-unchanged-UI, once per app, **zero Anthropic API calls** (compiled replay + OCR only, `grounder=None`).

## The central limitation (selection bias)

This corpus is **public, no-auth web apps only**. The real targets — enterprise desktop apps, Citrix/VDI, SSO-walled web apps — are exactly what this record-in-headless-chromium method **cannot reach**, so they are **unrepresented here**. Public apps are a *generalization proxy*, not the target population. Read every number below through that lens.

## Failure-mode deep dive (what the failures actually are)

These read from the per-app errors above plus manual investigation of each failing app; they are the honest interpretation behind the taxonomy counts.

**1. `identity_mismatch` is the dominant non-success mode (9 apps) — and it halts SAFELY.** Before every click the replayer re-reads the target's surrounding "context band" via OCR and compares it to the recorded band; a mismatch aborts rather than clicking a look-alike. That gate was tuned for dense EMR tables (where clicking the wrong row is a real safety bug). On text-dense *public web chrome* (nav bars, sidebars, search headers) the live band OCR differs from the recorded band by enough noise — a dropped glyph, a reflowed word, a CJK misread — to trip the gate on benign, correct targets (e.g. Bing observed `α` at coverage 0.00; Wikipedia 0.75; Selenium 0.96 and *still* aborted). This is **over-conservative for general web**, but it fails in the SAFE direction (halt, name the step, write nothing) — never a wrong click. It is the single biggest lever for lifting replay success on this corpus.

**2. Both `wrong_action`s are VACUOUS successes under an environment blocker — not harmful wrong-writes.** `duckduckgo_search`: the `html.duckduckgo.com/html/` endpoint returns *zero results* to headless chromium (bot mitigation), so the type+Enter flow's own (weak) postconditions pass while the goal is never reached. `petstore_expand`: an EU cookie-consent overlay (`ch2`) now intercepts pointer events, so the recorded click lands on the overlay and the expand "succeeds" vacuously. In BOTH the replayer reported success because the compiled postconditions for a single navigational click were too weak to notice that nothing happened — a real gap — but neither wrote a wrong value into a real field. The dangerous silent-wrong-WRITE mode (VALIDATION.md Track A) did not appear on this corpus.

**3. Compile is genuinely app-agnostic; the template rung carries unchanged-UI replay alone.** Nothing in record->compile was per-app tuned, yet every recorded demo compiled. Across the corpus every resolved anchored step used the `template` rung — the OCR/geometry/grounder rungs never fired, and there were zero heals and zero model calls. On *unchanged* UI the cheapest rung suffices; the lower rungs (and any model arm) only earn their keep under drift, which this study does not exercise.

## Verdict — product or bespoke?

Compile succeeded on 100% of recorded apps and 59% of replayed apps ended in a verified success, with 2 silent wrong-action(s).

The compiler is **general** in the mechanical sense: nothing in the record->compile path was per-app tuned, and it produced a runnable bundle for the large majority of apps it could record. Replay generalization is the harder axis — it degrades on dynamic content, pop-up/native widgets, and anti-bot/consent walls, but it degrades mostly by **halting honestly (safe_halt)**, not by acting wrongly.

Read against the selection bias: this is evidence the approach is **not per-app bespoke** on the class of apps it can reach, but it is **not yet evidence** for the auth-walled enterprise/desktop targets that are the actual product surface — those remain untested here.
